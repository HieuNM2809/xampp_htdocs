    Introduction to  HYCO

    Hybrid Career Exploration & Counselling (HYCO), is a hybrid counselling platform that not only helps students explore their skills and understands how these match different career options. But also connects them to professional experts in their area of interest.

    ​

    HYCO desires to catalyze the positive changes for people, society and nature. With features provided, it is considered a significant tool that helps students to discover their inner greatness, navigates to appropriate jobs, and overcome the adversities of Geographic location and low educational opportunity.

    Logo & Iconography

    The name - HYCO, genuinely comes from Hybrid Counselling which is short for Hybrid Career Exploration & Counselling. HYCO represents a modern counselling model which is devised by synergizing the power of technology and the experience of humans. 

    ​

    The primary colour of HYCO is purple which stands for cheerful–whimsical, playful and also associated with bravery. Purple is truly a meaningful colour which can deliver the spirit and value of our product to students perfectly.  In addition, three purple lines represent 3 missions that we'd love to bring to students "discover student inner greatness", "recommend relevant jobs" and "connect students to appropriate professional experts in the interest area"

    Applying Hooked Model

    Habit-Forming Product

    The Hook Model is a four-phase process that businesses can use to create products or services used habitually by customers. The goal is to result in voluntary, high-frequency engagement. At its core, the Hook Model is about creating a customer habit. It also seeks to connect a customer’s problem to a company’s solution with enough frequency to make the engagement an ongoing practice.

    ​

    t’s important to understand what drives customer behavior in order to build products that customers use habitually. With this knowledge, you can then create a significant competitive advantage for businesses. Product teams and product managers should use the Hook Model early in the product development process because it ensures that new products or services become habit-forming.
